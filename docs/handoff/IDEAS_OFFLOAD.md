## 1. Initial 7 Document Concepts (Archive)

1. **Intentio (Digital Detox Scheduling):** Scheduled offline blocks with social accountability pledges.
2. **Sanctuary (Digital-Free Work Spaces):** B2B marketplace for quiet zones, Faraday phone lockers, and no-WiFi work desks.
3. **Unplug Labs (Workplace Privacy Audit):** B2B consulting dashboard auditing corporate tech stacks for always-on culture.
4. **FocusFirst (Household Boundaries Controller):** Hardware/software router companion to enforce screen-free family hours.
5. **Silence Markets (Notification Economy Reversal):** Reverse marketplace paying users micro-rewards to mute notifications during deep work.
6. **Threshold (Information Diet Engine):** Rules-based app for managing notification diets and feed limits.
7. **Solitude Labs (Tech-Free Retreats):** Event directory for offline retreats and community dinners.

## 2. Pivot Rationale: De-clustering Megaplatforms

While the initial 7 concepts offered valid productivity tools, they treated disconnection as office utilities or lock timers. 

The **OGIS contest brief** explicitly highlighted the root crisis:
> *"In today's social media-driven environment, students experience a shift in relationships—**from 'narrow and deep' to 'broad and shallow' connections.**"*

### The Master Rationale:
* Modern social media has evolved into overwhelming **bloatware megaplatforms** housing news, shopping, work, and viral feeds.
* This environment reduces human relationships to passive **"friend/follower counters"** (500+ contacts) where interactions are transactional, broadcast-focused, and devoid of genuine warmth.
* **Kizuna (絆)** was created to de-cluster social media bloatware into a calm, intentional space built exclusively for **narrow and deep relationships**.
